3) Glossary

3D model: a digital representation of form, often with textures and materials.
Repository: organized storage and management space for files, metadata, and governance records.
Metadata: descriptive and technical information about files and objects.
Paradata: record of processing choices and assumptions.
Preservation master: highest quality retained version for long-term stewardship.
Access derivative: delivery copy made for public or classroom use.
Print derivative: print-ready output such as 3MF, STL, or G-code generated from the model package.
Fixity: evidence that files have not changed unexpectedly, usually tracked with checksums.
Checksum: short hash value used to verify file integrity.
Provenance: trace of where a file came from and how it changed through time.
Version: numbered snapshot of a file or package at a specific point in work.
Rights statement: legal status and ownership declaration applied to a digital asset.
Access level: release status such as public, internal, or restricted.
FAIR: principles that make data findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable.
CARE: principles emphasizing collective benefit, authority to control, responsibility, and ethics.
DOI: persistent identifier used for stable publication and citation.
