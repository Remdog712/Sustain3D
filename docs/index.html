Practical 3D Data Preservation

Sustain 3D is a lightweight, step-by-step framework for setting up and maintaining a small 3D repository. It is built for organizations that create 3D data but do not have a dedicated preservation department.

What this site does

Sustain 3D translates preservation research and community standards into practical steps that small institutions can realistically implement.

1. Defines a complete workflow

From capture planning to long-term storage, the workflow identifies “Preservation Intervention Points” so important files and decisions are not lost at project end.

2. Provides repository structure

Folder hierarchies, inventory templates, and naming conventions that prevent the most common failure mode: unstructured files on a hard drive.

3. Separates preservation from access

Clear distinction between preservation masters and web-ready derivatives, reducing accidental loss of high-fidelity data.

4. Integrates FAIR + CARE principles

Technical sustainability (FAIR) and ethical governance (CARE) are embedded into documentation, access levels, and reuse policies.

5. Scales by capacity

Each step includes Good / Better / Best tiers so institutions can match preservation practices to their time and resources.

6. Remains tool-agnostic

Sustain 3D does not depend on proprietary systems. It emphasizes transparency, open formats, and portable documentation.

The core idea in three steps

  1. Structure your files. Organize projects intentionally before they sprawl.
  2. Document your decisions. Record capture context, software, and processing steps.
  3. Protect your masters. Maintain preservation copies separate from access derivatives.
Preservation is not about keeping everything forever. It is about keeping the right things, in the right way, with enough context to remain meaningful.
