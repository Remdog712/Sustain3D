Minimum metadata (small-institution baseline)

Metadata is how someone else (or future you) can understand what the asset is, where it came from, and what they’re allowed to do with it.

Minimum fields (recommended)

object_id
title
creator/operator
date_created
physical_object_or_site (what was scanned)
capture_method (photogrammetry / LiDAR / structured light)
device(s)
software + version(s)
units + scale reference
file list (what belongs to this package)
rights + license
access level (public / internal / restricted)
preservation master format + location
derivatives format + location
processing notes (paradata)

Paradata: document the decisions

A 3D model is shaped by cleanup, meshing, decimation, and export settings. Paradata is the record of those decisions.

Paradata checklist (plain language)

  • What did you remove or change (noise cleanup, hole filling, smoothing)?
  • Did you decimate or simplify? If yes, how aggressively?
  • Did you change textures (resolution, compression, re-bake)?
  • What exports were produced and why (preservation vs access)?
DPC 3D preservation note:
“need to preserve a lot of additional metadata…”

3D preservation often requires device/tool context beyond the mesh file.

FAIR and CARE (what they mean here)

FAIR

FAIR authors (Wilkinson et al., 2016):
“Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reusability…”

For Sustain 3D, FAIR means: consistent IDs, clear metadata, documented formats, and stated reuse conditions.

CARE

RDA (CARE Principles one-pager):
“authority to control such data be empowered.”

CARE matters when objects involve community authority, cultural protocols, or sensitive materials.

Practical use

  • FAIR: make the asset discoverable and usable with enough description and structure.
  • CARE: clearly record restrictions, community authority, and ethical conditions for reuse.
